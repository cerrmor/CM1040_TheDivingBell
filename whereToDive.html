5 Must Dive Locations

The world is a vast place with many amazing dive sites to explore. But there are a few sites, that stand out above the rest for their ease of accessibility and diversity of life. So if you are into diving, these dive sites should be on your bucket list!

A Quick Note.

The environment is changing faster than we would like. Weather it be from a careless industry. or our own mismanagement of our selves and the items we bring with us. So, to avoid forever damaging these delicate environments. Please when exploring, pack out what you packed in and leave the spot better then when you found it so it can be enjoyed for year to come.

1. Micronesia

Micronesia is group of around 2100 islands in the western Pacific Ocean, near Indonesia and the Philippines. The area boasts WW2 and WW1 wrecks that can be dove, as well as coral gardens and over 1300 different species of fish. All of this makes the area loaded with different dive experiences and so requires multiple visits to take it all in. The common list of top two dive spots are.

2. Egypt

Egypt is an amazing place to travel given its awe-inspiring history and historical monuments. But something maybe less well known, is that it is also one of the best places to go diving in the world. The Red Sea is host to clear warm water, ranging in temperatures from 22 ºC - 30 ºC depending on the time of year. Which makes extended periods of swimming typically very comfortable, and the abundance of marine life greater than other locations in the world.

Two dives to not miss out on if you go are.

Indonesia

Indonesia waters are fill with approximately 25% of the worlds fish species and 72% of the coral species. This makes it a top diving spot for beginners and professional alike. To make matters even better the climate is tropical and the water temperature is fairly stable around 26 ºC making extended periods of swimming very comfortable.

Hawaii

The waters around the Hawaiian Islands are often counted among the very best in the world for diving. There are frequent sightings of large animals and turtles, and a number of huge wrecks for exploring. To make it even better the waters are crystal-clear making for some of the best diving anywhere.

Chile

Chile has over 4000 km of coast line on the South Pacific Ocean. This leaves a vast area for available dive sites, which includes everything from sunken wrecks to sunken archeological sites. This means there is a little something for everyone see. The coastline being as vast as it is, also means that you may not see any other divers other then ones you are with, which can be very nice.
