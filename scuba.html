How long has scuba been around?

The first full time SCUBA system was invented in 1825 by William James an English man, however the air belt only stored 450 psi of air and it is not known if this system was actually used for diving.

Over the next 195 years to modern day, diving equipment has been steadily improving. Both in length of time one can stay underwater and simplicity of gear. What do I need to know to go diving?

Diving is an incredibly rewarding activity and almost any body of water can be dove in. But there are a few key things to remember if you decide to take up this sport.

  1. First Make sure you have the correct training/experience. There are many great schools that will train and certify you, such as PADI.
  2. Second never go scuba diving alone! Almost every incident of a diver losing their life, or getting seriously injured occurred when the diver was by themselves and without help.
  3. Third find out the possible hazards from an experienced guide before diving in a new area.
  4. Fourth make sure your equipment is well serviced and/or sourced from a reputable company.
  5. Fifth test your gear before you get in the water. it is life support equipment and it just has to work.

I'm a beginner where should I start?

So, you have decided that you want to try this amazing sport. You have all the gear, or know where to get it and you have gotten certified. How do you find people to go diving with and where do you go?

You can always check out the guided tours in these areas, they are a great place to begin, learn and meet like minded people.

1. British Virgin Islands

Most of the diving in this region is less then 80 feet or 26 meters deep. Meaning the risk of going to deep is all but eliminated. The coast line is also quite sheltered so not much for surge currents or other such hazards. Finally, there are several wrecks and reefs that can be safely explored making this a great place to begin.
