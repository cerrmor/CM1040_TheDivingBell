How is Commercial Diving different from scuba?

Fundamentally Commercial Diving and Scuba Diving are very similar in that they both take place underwater. However, the two activities begin to show their differences right away. Almost all commercial diving uses surface supplied air delivered, by a supply line called the umbilical. This means divers can stay underwater longer and go deeper then with classic scuba diving. Another difference is that the diver has two-way communication with the surface at all times. finally, what is most likely the biggest difference is, a commercial diver can do something called saturation diving. Saturation diving is the process, where a diver is pressurised in a hyperbaric chamber to the depth of water that the work needs to be completed at. The diver then stays at that pressure until the job is done or their work rotation is done.

How do I become a Commercial Diver?

There are two methods to becoming a commercial diver. The first and arguably the easiest method is to attend a collage like these recognised by the Diver Certification Board of Canada.

  • Canadian Working Divers Institute
  • B.C. College of Diving
  • Commercial Diving Institute of Canada

For a full listing of schools recognised in Canada please click here

While the collages are an excellent way to learn the necessary skills, they are not the only way. The second route is to progress through the different dive certifications on an individual basis. Starting with a basic recreational open water certification. However, this can be a very slow and costly route.

What kind of work can I expect to be doing?

Commercial divers can be used in almost every industry, so it is hard to say specifically what you will be doing. But this is also a good thing in that no job will ever be the same. Here is a short list of possible jobs, though there are many more.

  • Off shore Oil Rig Diver
  • Underwater Welder
  • Scientific Sample Recovery
  • Underwater Photographer
  • Underwater Equipment Inspector
  • Pipe Fitter

What does a Commercial Diver earn?

on average commercial diver's start earning around 52 000 a year but this can easily increase to over 100 000 or more, as you increase in skill and your name gets better known in the industry.
